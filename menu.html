Monday- Mexican Food

Mexican Food is culinary norms vary widely based on income level and social class. The diet of working-class Mexicans Food includes staples such as corn or wheat tortillas, along with beans, rice, tomatoes, chili peppers and chorizo, a type of pork sausage. Empanadas, which are handheld pasty pockets, can contain savory or sweet fillings. Many Mexicans love spicy foods full of heat.

Saturday- Soul Food
October 13, 2013

Soul food is a term used for African American cuisine. The phrase originated in the South during the Civil Rights Movement in the 1960's when it was common to use "soul" to describe black culture; for example, "soul music" and "soul man." Traditional soul food is flavorful because of the heavy use of seasonings.
